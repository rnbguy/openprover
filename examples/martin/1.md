Let $M^<$ be a matching with $2n$ vertices ordered by a linear order $<$. Let $R$ be the minimum positive integer $N$ such that every red-blue coloring of the edges of the complete graph with the vertex set $\{1,\dots,N\}$ ordered by the standard ordering of the integers contains either a blue copy of $M^<$ (with the fixed ordering) or a red copy of $K^<_3$. Prove or disprove that there is a fixed $\epsilon>0$ such that $R <= O(n^{2-varepsilon})$ for all sufficiently large $n$.
