For a positive integer $k$, a mixed strategy $x_i$ of player $i$ on the action set $[m]=]{1,\dots,m\}$ is $k$-uniform if there are pure strategies $a_1,\dots,a_k$ of $i$ such that $x_i = (\sum_{j=1}^k a_j)/k$. Let $G$ be a normal-form game of $n$ players, where each one of them has 2 actions. Prove that for every $\epsilon>0$ there is $k=k(\epsilon)$ such that $G$ has a $k$-uniform $\epsilon$-Nash equilibrium.
Note that $k$ should only depend on $\epsilon$, not e.g. on $n$ (number of players).
